PJJ Story

PJJ or what is commonly called Distance Learning began when it was announced that it was a two-week holiday to prepare for the 12th grade National Examination. I didn't think about this long online learning because at that time I thought it was only two weeks off. When it was announced that online learning was taking place, I was clearly shocked because I am not used to studying from home and with a mediocre quota because my house has not yet installed wifi. I also haven't moved my things from the boarding house and my father who works outside the city can't take me to pick up books. So I learn only with the internet, I also search for modules only on the internet. I study the same as at school, except that sometimes the time to start learning may not match the schedule, it can be earlier or later in the day. If it's early in the morning, my account is stuck because I usually wake up and take a shower and wearing a uniform and then zooming in, when I'm late I usually don't take a shower right then, but after. But when the start time is noon, I usually help with the housework first, take a shower and then go to school.

I also don't have breakfast all the time but sometimes I eat when the zoom hasn't started, my breakfast is only limited to drinking milk and cereal. After a break before entering the fifth hour lesson I will take a prayer break and eat rice. Then after returning to the lesson, if there is no work, I immediately change clothes and take a nap. This was repeated until school was over and on weekends I only focused on watching movies, playing, or cooking cakes at home. When PJJ also has many obstacles. Often the internet becomes the number one barrier because if there is no internet everything will not be accessible. In addition, I am a careless child and easily forget things, so I like to forget to turn in or submit assignments, so my grades are reduced because they think I didn't collect assignments. Luckily I have a friend who at school we sit at the same table, so I will do my homework with him and he also often reminds me of assignments and vice versa.
